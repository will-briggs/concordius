# A Structural Reading of Eastern Traditions

The readings in this section apply the Concordius framework to Eastern philosophical, cosmological, and divinatory traditions. These traditions arrived at their structural descriptions by routes entirely independent of the Western mathematical and theological sources from which the framework is primarily derived — which makes them among the most evidentially significant convergences in the series.

Independent convergence is the strongest available form of concordance evidence: when a tradition with no access to the framework's sources identifies the same structural feature, by a completely different method, the convergence is evidence that both are tracking something structurally real. The readings here document that convergence precisely.
