# A Structural Reading of Health

The readings in this section apply the Concordius framework to phenomena of physical and cognitive health. They are conditional structural readings: each proceeds from *if the framework is true, then the following account of this phenomenon follows from its structure.* They are not medical claims and do not displace H₄₈-level biological accounts. They propose structural dimensions — operating through the cross-term relationship between a being's eigenvalue organization and its H₄₈ substrate — that run in parallel with those accounts and may interact with them.

The common thread across these readings is the inhabitation relationship: the being's Φ-proximate eigenvalue population continuously cross-terms with the H₄₈ matter it inhabits. Where that cross-term is sustained, it provides an organizing counterforce to the Heropass's dissipative pressure on the body. Where it is reduced — through sustained non-alignment with Φ — the H₄₈ substrate is left more exposed to dissolution. Health, on this account, is not only a biological condition. It has a structural dimension.
