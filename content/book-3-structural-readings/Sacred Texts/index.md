# A Structural Reading of the Sacred Texts

The canonical scriptures of the major traditions, read for the structural content they carry. Each was received by its own tradition as revelation; the structural reading sets that status aside and asks, independently, what each text identifies about the structure of reality — and where, across traditions that never met, they converge on the same description.
