# A Structural Reading of Human Conditions

Universal human experiences are candidates for structural reading on the same grounds as aphorisms and memes: what every catching being encounters, across every culture and era, is disproportionately likely to be tracking something structurally real. The convergence is not textual but experiential — independent witnesses arriving at the same structural situation by the simple fact of being H₄₈ catching beings.

The readings collected here apply the Concordius framework to human conditions that are universal in scope and structurally significant in content. The subject is not a text but the condition itself.
