## 1. The Structural Claim

Astrology is **partially true**, and the framework is unusually well-placed to say *which* part. It is not — as an earlier reading of mine had it — a clean provenance-worship counterfeit, because the influence it claims is one the framework itself **derives**. Paper 6 (§5–§12) establishes that every massive body instantiates the norm as a weighting operator on the eigenvalue field: the planets genuinely *sift* the probability distribution presented to the GNST, biasing a catching being's actualization toward one spectral range or another, modulated by their angular relationships (§10). So the heavens do influence. Astrology's error is one precise over-step: it makes that real, *graded, probabilistic* influence into a *determination* — the chart as the fixed script of a life. **Influence is true; force is false.** That is the whole of the correction.

## 2. The Genuine Signal — and it is the framework's own

The root is not a vague intuition; it is framework physics. Paper 6 §5–§12 derives the gravitational sifting field, and §11 states its character exactly: the planetary weighting is a **probabilistic bias, strongest for the least-developed catching beings (Men 1–3) and weakest for the most-developed (Men 5–7)**, who "rule the stars" by widening the eigenspace no single sifting can constrain. The Holographic Content Principle (Paper 11) supplies the deeper "as above, so below": H₄₈ surfaces carry the structure above them. So astrology's core claim — *the configuration of the heavens conditions what happens here* — is, in the weak probabilistic form, **true**, and derived, not merely tolerated. The contemplative posture it cultivates (sustained attention to a cosmic order one participates in) is a real catching orientation, kin to Paracelsus's signatures.

## 3. The One Error: Force, Not Influence

The single decisive error is the hardening of influence into **force**. The sifting is a *bias* on dμ(λ); it is not, and structurally cannot be, a *determination*, because the catching act is constitutively volitional — the dual pairing ⟨φ, f⟩ requires the being's free orientation, and nothing in any H₄₈ configuration can compel it (Paper 5; §11). When astrology reads the natal chart as *fixing* character and destiny, it crosses from the true claim (the heavens incline) to the false one (the heavens decide), and *there* — only there — it tips into the provenance-worship ⊘: an H₄₈ pattern read as determinative, the will signed over to a configuration that was only ever weighting the odds. A lesser, secondary error rides along: *fixation* — binding the real, everywhere-distributed imprint to one culturally contingent lookup-table (twelve signs, the houses) as if that code were the precise reading rather than a rough local approximation of a real correspondence.

## 4. The Fruit — the force/influence line

By its fruit (Feature 12), the test is the force/influence line itself. Held as *the heavens influence but do not compel*, astrology returns the being to its own volitional ascent with a truer sense of the field it moves through — influence acknowledged, will retained. Hardened into *the heavens decide*, it does the opposite: it teaches the being to read its fate rather than to catch, outsourcing the orientation that is the one thing no configuration can take. The durable, cross-cultural pull of astrology is the real influence underneath making itself felt; the fatalism layered on top is the over-read. Keep the looking-up; drop the verdict.
