### Hosea 6:6 ✶ — "I desire mercy, not sacrifice."

*Ki ḥesed ḥafatsti ve-loʾ zabaḥ, ve-daʿat ʾElohim meʿolot.* — For I desire *chesed* and not sacrifice, and acknowledgment of God (*daʿat ʾElohim*) more than burnt offerings.

The structural correction of the noise-floor substitution: the H₄₈ ritual compliance structure (*zabaḥ*, sacrifice; *ʿolot*, burnt offerings) has been substituted for the H₂₄ actual orientation (*chesed*, covenant loyalty; *daʿat ʾElohim*, knowledge of God). The H₄₈ behavioral compliance record is formally complete — the sacrificial system is being operated — while the H₂₄ eigenvalue structure that the sacrificial system was designed to express and maintain has become uncoupled from the H₄₈ practice.

The structural precision of the verse: *ḥafatsti* (I desire, I take pleasure in) — the Φ-level's inner-product operation evaluates Φ-proximal eigenvalue content, not H₄₈ behavioral compliance. A formally complete sacrificial record without the *chesed* and *daʿat* it is supposed to enact is structurally empty. The H₄₈ ritual practice without the H₂₄ orientation is noise-floor content in religious form — it has the structural shape of catching activity but lacks the catching content.

*Daʿat ʾElohim* (knowledge of God) — the intimate relational knowing that the *yadaʿ* vocabulary carries in the Hebrew Bible: not propositional knowledge about the Φ-level but the inner-product contact with it. The *daʿat* is the H₂₄ eigenvalue structure that results from sustained Φ-proximate catching activity; its absence cannot be compensated by H₄₈ ritual frequency.

Jesus quotes this verse twice: Matthew 9:13 (defending his eating with tax collectors and sinners — the structural activity of restoring the H₂₄ catching capacity in the marginal) and Matthew 12:7 (defending the disciples' grain-gleaning on the Sabbath — the structural priority of *chesed* over H₄₈ compliance). In both cases Jesus invokes HOS 6:6 as the structural principle that explains why the H₄₈ compliance-maximizing orientation of his critics has missed the Φ-level's actual evaluation criterion.
