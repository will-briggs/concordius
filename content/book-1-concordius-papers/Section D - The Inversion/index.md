# Section D — The Inversion

*Papers D0–D7*

---

Every note of the foregoing octave can be run backwards. Section D is the **Fa** of the larger octave — the photographic negative of the Good, the True, and the Beautiful, read in the men and the events of the twentieth century. Three men invert the three transcendentals on the moral axis (Hitler, the Good; Stalin, the True; Mao, the Beautiful); three events invert the bivector virtues (the Holocaust, the Great Purge, the Great Leap Forward); and between them the Demagogue and the Crowd, each culpable in their own measure. The descent runs down through Lucifer and the Fall to Evil itself — the hinge at which the work must leave the system entirely, which the next section does. Each reading carries its balance: *there but for the grace of God.*
