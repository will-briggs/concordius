# Feature C — The Boundary

---

*Faces C0–C3: derivation, its types, and where it stops. Warrant labelled by how it was earned; the edge of what logic can derive, past which method must change; the cost of every assumption you must reach across that edge; and the consistency without which the whole closure collapses.*

The third object is the **typed deductive closure** — the structure of warrant. Its faces hold that warrant is not a single scalar but a *type* (derived, inferred, posited); that the deductive type has an *edge* you must name rather than walk past; that crossing the edge has a *cost*, paid one unexplained assumption at a time; and that the whole closure must be *consistent*, since a single contradiction makes everything derivable and the edge meaningless. Type, edge, cost, consistency.

## Formal Statement (Concordius Framework)

The object is the **deductive closure** Cl(𝒫) of the primitives and its type structure. Its faces: **C0** warrant is typed (deductive / convergent / testimonial — collapsing the types is an OVERCLAIM); **C1** the boundary ∂Cl(𝒫) where the deductive type ends and convergence takes over; **C2** the cost of exogenous inputs (minimize k by the MDL / Occam factor — each posited 'miracle' is a free parameter crossing ∂); and **C3** consistency (by *ex falso*, an inconsistent closure derives everything, voiding the typing). C3 is the precondition of the other three.

*Tier: **derivation** for C0/C2/C3; **criterion** for C1 (membership in Cl(𝒫) is undecidable in general).*
