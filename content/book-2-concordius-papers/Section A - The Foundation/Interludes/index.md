# Interludes — Adam Reads the Foundation

*The connective tissue. Adam Atomman — eighty-three, nuclear physicist, three years a Jesuit seminarian who left because he "didn't know enough yet to promise this" — sits in the gaps between the papers and takes the Foundation apart looking for the seam. Each interlude is keyed to the paper it introduces; read them in sequence with the papers themselves.*

- **[[Adam A0: An Unexpected Letter]]** — the envelope arrives among the bills; he takes it for a prank from a former student and carries it to the study with the red pencil. *(Opens the Foundation, before Paper A0.)*
- **[[Adam A1: An Unexpected Visitor]]** — Joe drops by; Adam explains the Gelfand triple on the back of the envelope and names the Gödel problem the paper has walked into; then, alone, the seminary circle the reading reopened. *(After Paper A0, into Paper A1.)*
